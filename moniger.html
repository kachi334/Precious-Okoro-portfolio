Elevating User Experience: Moniger Mobile App and Website

Role UX Copywriter, Researcher and Content Strategist
Timeline 2023 – 2026
Industry Fintech
Tools Figma, Google Docs, Miro
Content StrategyUX WritingFintechMobileWeb
The Problem

Moniger simplifies financial management. Users link multiple bank accounts, track budgets, and manage subscriptions from a single view.

Most people arrived at the product already overwhelmed, manually tracking accounts across different banks with no consolidated picture of their money.

The product solved a real problem. The risk was explaining it in language dense enough to sound credible and simple enough to earn trust before anyone linked an account.

Key Decisions
01

Anchor Copy in Real Language

Surveys and interviews with people managing multiple bank accounts surfaced the exact words they used for their pain points. Those words, not internal terminology, became the product's vocabulary.

02

Let Content Lead the Design

Copy shaped the design team's wireframes and prototypes, not the reverse. Every screen was built around how the information needed to be understood first.

03

Govern the Voice, Not Just the Words

A content style guide and A/B-tested copy gave the team a system for tone, voice, and messaging that held up as new features shipped.

The Website

Every functional website depends on one thing in its hero sections: a simplified voice.

Across Moniger's web pages, home, virtual cards, budgeting, finance manager, subscriptions, and account aggregation, the goal was clear, consistent microcopy pulled from research, not guesswork.

Before that copy touched a single wireframe, I ran a closed card-sorting exercise to define content hierarchy. The information architecture reflected how users actually grouped these features, not how the org chart did.

Content Systems and Documentation

The engagement went beyond shipping copy for a single release. I built the infrastructure that let Moniger's team keep creating strong content without me in the room.

  • Help center documentation, mapped to each stage of the user journey, from account setup and bank linking to budgeting and subscription management, so users could resolve questions the moment they hit them.
  • Blog content supporting the product's positioning and helping users understand features beyond the app itself.
  • A content system and style guide governing tone, voice, and messaging across mobile, web, and support content, so the product stayed consistent as new features shipped.
The Mobile App

The mobile app carried real complexity: accounts, budgets, and subscriptions, all needing one consistent flow.

I introduced a splash screen and used the Nickel Tour approach for onboarding, a multi-step progress indicator that always shows users exactly where they stand.

Research surfaced two distinct segments: people juggling multiple bank accounts, and people who wanted to track spending on the go without opening a spreadsheet. Every piece of copy was tested against both before it shipped.

The Results
25% Increase in conversions
8% Reduction in onboarding drop-off
  • 2+ year content-first engagement across mobile app and website
  • One content style guide governing every touchpoint
  • Plain-language copy replacing internal financial jargon
  • Self-serve help center content mapped to the full user journey
Takeaway

Complexity in a product does not have to become complexity in the copy. On Moniger, the words carried the weight so the interface never had to.
